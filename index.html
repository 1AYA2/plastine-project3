Lake Tiberias

Lake Tiberias is a freshwater lake located in the north of Palestine. It is surrounded by hills on all sides. Mount Erbil is located to its west, and the Syrian Golan Heights are located to its east. It is the second lowest lake in the world after the Dead Sea. It is also called the Sea of Galilee. Through it the Jordan River flows, and in the period extending between 1948 AD and 1967 AD, the lake was surrounded on its northeastern side by the ceasefire line between Syria and Israel.

The Dead Sea

The Dead Sea is a landlocked sea located in the southwest of the continent of Asia, bordered by Jordan to the east and Palestine to the west. The Dead Sea is considered the lowest water spot on the surface of the Earth, as it drops approximately 400 meters below sea level. The length of the Dead Sea is about 67 km. Its width at its widest point is about 18 km, and the Jordan River is considered its main tributary. The Dead Sea is distinguished by its highly saline waters and its rich mud with a high percentage of minerals of high health value.

The city of Bisan

The city of Bisan is one of the oldest Palestinian cities, which was called in the past (Beit Shean), which means the House of Tranquility. It is a name of Canaanite origin, while the Greeks and in the Hellenistic era called it (Keolis), and there is another name given to it (Nissa). The history of this city dates back to BC, when people used to live in tents made of animal skins. In the fifth century BC, Beit Shean was under Egyptian rule, as traces of the Egyptians are still clearly visible.
